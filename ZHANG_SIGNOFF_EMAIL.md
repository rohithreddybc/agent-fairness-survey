**Subject:** Draft to share — *Fairness and Equity in LLM-Based Agents* (your thoughts welcome)

Dear Dr. Zhang,

I've put together a full draft of the survey we discussed, **"Fairness and Equity in
LLM-Based Agents: A Taxonomic Survey"** (attached). I think of it as the agentic
successor to your *Fairness in Large Language Models* taxonomy: where that work maps
bias in LLMs that **answer**, this one maps bias in LLMs that **act** (selecting
tools, accumulating memory, delegating across agents, planning, drifting over long
horizons).

A few things you might find worth a look:

- **An original framework, not just a literature review.** The paper introduces the
  **Bias Conduction Framework**: a two-axis model of where bias enters the agent
  pipeline and how it is transformed at each step (attenuate / preserve / amplify /
  generate), with formal definitions and several derived propositions.
- **202 references**, each verified against arXiv/DOI, organized by agent component.
- **A small original pilot study** grounding the framework, written up honestly with
  its limitations.

I would be honored to have you as **senior author**, and I'd genuinely value your
read on the framing and the open-problems section. There's no rush on my end. I'm
happy to work at whatever pace suits you, to revise wherever you'd like, and to defer
entirely to your judgment on timing and venue (I'd lean toward an arXiv preprint at
some point, perhaps with SIGKDD Explorations or ACM Computing Surveys to follow, but
I'm very open to your preference).

Whenever you have a chance to look, I'd love to hear your thoughts. Thank you for the
guidance that shaped this direction.

Warm regards,
Rohith
